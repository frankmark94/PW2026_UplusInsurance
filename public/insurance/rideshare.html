Rideshare & Gig Driver Coverage

Drive for Uber or Lyft?
Make sure you're covered.

Standard auto policies were written for commuters, not rideshare drivers. When you're logged into a driver app, your personal coverage may be suspended — leaving you exposed. Here's what every rideshare driver needs to know.

The three rideshare coverage periods

Your coverage changes based on what you're doing in the app. Most drivers don't realize this.

Period 1

App off

Driving personally — commuting, errands, or just for fun. You're not logged into any rideshare app.

Fully covered by your personal policy
Period 2

App on, waiting for a ride

Logged into Uber or Lyft, driving around waiting to be matched with a passenger. This is the riskiest period.

Limited coverage — major gap
Period 3

Passenger in the car

You've accepted a ride and either en route to pick up or driving a passenger to their destination.

Excluded — personal policy suspended

The rideshare gap — and why it matters

Most standard auto policies include a rideshare exclusion (commonly cited as Endorsement E-441). That means when you're logged into a rideshare app, your personal insurer may deny claims for accidents, injuries, or damage. And while Uber and Lyft carry contingent coverage, it typically has high deductibles ($2,500+) and only applies in Period 3. You could be on the hook for thousands.

Common rideshare questions

Have a specific scenario in mind? Our Virtual Assistant can walk through your policy details.

Does Uber's or Lyft's insurance cover me?

Partially. Uber and Lyft offer contingent liability coverage, but it only kicks in during Period 3 (passenger in car) and typically has high deductibles. Periods 1 and 2 are where most drivers have the biggest gaps.

What about DoorDash, Instacart, or other delivery apps?

Delivery driving triggers similar exclusions on most personal auto policies. A commercial rideshare policy typically covers both rideshare and delivery, while rideshare endorsements vary — it's worth confirming with our Virtual Assistant.

Will adding rideshare coverage raise my rates?

Yes, but the increase is typically modest for a rideshare endorsement — far less than paying out-of-pocket for an uninsured accident. Your rate depends on your driving history, location, and how many hours you drive.

What if I only drive rideshare occasionally?

Even occasional drivers are exposed to the same coverage gaps. A rideshare endorsement is usually the right fit for part-time drivers — low monthly cost, complete protection.
